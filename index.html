Despite continued oversight by the Trump administration, Armstrong, Shipman, and Mnookin have a history of large donations to Democrats

Jennifer Mnookin, Columbia’s next president, has donated over $5,900 to Democratic campaigns since 2008.

By Pedro Meerbaum

Graphic by Emilia Lebeau

February 16, 2026

Audrea Chen / Photo Editor

Columbia’s current, upcoming, and former presidents all share one thing in common: an extensive history of Democratic campaign donations.

Spectator reviewed Federal Election Commission data on incoming University President Jennifer Mnookin, her husband Joshua Dienstag, acting University President Claire Shipman, CC ’86, SIPA ’94, and former interim University President Katrina Armstrong.

The three University leaders, who all lean Democratic, will have served at least part of their tenure under a Republican president.

Mnookin has donated over $5,900 to Democratic campaigns since 2008. With donations to former presidents Barack Obama, CC ’83, and Joe Biden, among other Democratic political figures, Mnookin’s donation history mirrors that of previous University presidents. However, her donations pale in comparison to Shipman’s, whose donations total over $134,000 to Democratic campaigns since 2016.

Armstrong has donated over $3,600 to Democrats. With the exception of Armstrong’s $500 donation to Congressman Adriano Espaillat’s (D-N.Y.) 2022 campaign, none of the donations occurred while the presidents were working at the University. Former University President Minouche Shafik, who lives in London, has no public history of political donations in the United States.

Mnookin first donated to the Obama campaign in 2008, when she served as the vice dean of faculty and research at the University of California, Los Angeles. She donated to Hillary Clinton in 2016, Biden in 2020, and an assortment of Democratic congressional candidates through ActBlue, the official Democratic donation platform. Mnookin has not donated to any candidate since 2021 and has served as the chancellor of the University of Wisconsin-Madison since 2022.

While at UW-Madison, Mnookin faced significant Republican pushback. She was lambasted by Republican legislators as an “out-of-touch elite” when she was first appointed to the position. As chancellor, Mnookin signed a deal with Republican lawmakers to limit the University’s diversity, equity, and inclusion program in order to receive $800 million in state funding.

During her tenure as chancellor of UW-Madison, Mnookin spearheaded the Wisconsin Exchange, a program aimed at increasing dialogue on campus across the political spectrum. In an interview with The Madison Federalist, a UW-Madison student newspaper with a conservative slant, Mnookin argued for diversity in faculty members’ political affiliation.

Joshua Dienstag, Mnookin’s husband, has also donated to several Democratic candidates, totaling over $3,000 in congressional elections.

Dienstag is a professor of political science at UW-Madison, with a research focus on political theory. Dienstag’s donations all occurred during his time as a professor at UCLA, where he taught from 2005 to 2024. He last donated in 2022.

Mnookin and Dienstag’s combined contributions amount to $9,461.

Of Shipman’s $134,390 in donations, $63,300 went to Kamala Harris’s 2024 presidential campaign. Shipman donated to the campaign on three separate occasions, one of which amounted to $50,000, her largest overall donation to date.

Shipman’s first donation was in 2016 to Hillary Clinton’s presidential campaign. Her last contribution was an October 2024 donation to various state Democratic parties, just five months before she became acting University president.

Armstrong, who in August 2024 stepped in as interim University president following Shafik’s resignation, also has a history of donating to Democrats. Her political donations span from February 2008, when she was a professor at the University of Pennsylvania, to March 2022, when she became CEO of Columbia University Irving Medical Center. The majority of her contributions took place from 2013 to early 2022, while she worked as a physician in chief at Massachusetts General Hospital.

Despite her continued contributions, Armstrong’s donations equal only 2.7 percent of Shipman’s.

Shipman surpasses Mnookin, Dienstag, and Armstrong in contributions to Democratic committees and funds, as well as to presidential candidates. While Shipman primarily focused on presidential campaigns, Armstrong donated more than her to congressional candidates.

Shipman’s political donations to presidential candidates are over 26 times larger than those of Mnookin, Dienstag, and Armstrong combined

Despite her large contributions to general Democratic party funds and presidential candidates, Shipman does not donate to congressional candidates at all. In all categories, Mnookin, Dienstag, and Armstrong’s donations are relatively similar, never varying by more than $1,750.

“University employees, like all private citizens, are free to participate fully in the political process, including by making donations using their personal funds to candidates and causes of their choosing,” a University official told Spectator. “Such activities are always undertaken in one’s private capacity and not on behalf of the University, which is prohibited from participating in partisan activities that support political candidates”.

Mnookin is set to assume the presidency on July 1. She will be the third person to lead the University a year and half into Trump’s second presidential term.
